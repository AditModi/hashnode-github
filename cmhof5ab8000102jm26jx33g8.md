## **Imagine You’re Expanding Your Business to New Cities**

If you were opening stores in different cities, you’d first want to know:

* Does this mall have the exact type of store location I need?
    
* Will the city grant the right permits?
    
* Can I bring my equipment here, or do I have to buy something different?
    

**Cloud deployments across AWS Regions work the same way.**  
Not every Region has the same services, APIs, or instance types.

And historically, figuring that out meant:

* digging through docs
    
* checking pricing pages
    
* asking “Is this available in…” on random Slack channels
    

### AWS just solved this with:

**✅ AWS Capabilities by Region**

It’s basically **your global store directory for the cloud.**

## **A Simple 5-Step Regional Planning Checklist**

Use this with the tool:

1. **List all required AWS services + specific features** your app needs.
    
2. **Check each target Region** using AWS Capabilities by Region.
    
3. Note the status:
    
    * **Available** = good to go
        
    * **Planning** = possible delay risk
        
    * **Not Expanding** = pick another Region or refactor
        
4. Confirm **CloudFormation support** for your templates.
    
5. Document **launch timeline expectations** for business stakeholders.
    

This prevents:

* surprise delays,
    
* unexpected code changes,
    
* and the dreaded *“Wait… this Region doesn’t support that??”* moment.
